The Saga Begins

A warrior lost.
A legend reborn.

In the dying embers of feudal Japan, when the Mongol tide swept through the islands of Tshuma, one samurai was left for dead — his clan slaughtered, his honor shattered, his name erased from the living world.

But death, it seems, had other plans. Rising from the battlefield mist as something neither man nor myth, Jin Tsuru became what the enemy feared most: a ghost, a shadow, a force of silent retribution.

He would use every forbidden art, every dishonored technique, every shadow and silence the night could offer — not because it was righteous, but because it was necessary.

The samurai fights in the open light of day. The ghost moves where the light cannot follow — and strikes before the darkness even knows he is there.

— The Chronicles of Tshuma, Vol. I
